Hi, I'm Himanshi.

UX Designer · Gurugram, India

"Good design shouldn't shout — it should whisper. The best interfaces are the ones you stop noticing because they get out of your way."

Who I am

I'm a UX designer with 3+ years of experience building scalable, data-driven products. I came into design through code — I spent five months as a backend developer before realising the part I loved was the seam between the system and the human using it. That dual lens shows up in my work: I think in components, but I design for the messy edges where real people live.

Today I lead end-to-end design at Dancing Numbers — research, IA, flows, hi-fi, design QA, and the design system that holds it all together.

What I do

I design for SaaS and data-heavy interfaces — the kind of products where users come to get a job done, not to be entertained. That means tight workflows, sensible defaults, and screens that respect your attention. I work closely with PMs and engineers; I prototype to make trade-offs visible early; I obsess about empty states.

Recent wins: reduced onboarding drop-offs 25% through flow optimisation, lifted daily active users 15% with a dashboard redesign, and cut design hand-off friction 20% by rebuilding the team's component library.

Off the clock

You'll find me hunting for coffee shops that get the foam right, re-organising my Figma libraries for the third time this week, or on a slow train somewhere in the hills. I keep a running list of tiny UX details I love — receipts, transit cards, museum signage — and study them like other people study films.

projects › fintrack

FinTrack — Fintech analytics dashboard

Redesigned a finance ops dashboard for a B2B SaaS where analysts were drowning in 14-column tables. Built a hierarchy of summary → drill-down → raw, and a saved-view system so each role lands on what they need first.

Time-to-insight: 8 min → under 2 min SaaS Dashboard Data UX Design System

Role

Lead UX · research, IA, hi-fi, QA

Problem

Power users could find anything but newcomers churned in the first week. The home screen showed everything to everyone.

Process

Ran 9 contextual interviews across 3 roles, mapped current-state journeys, prototyped 3 IA directions in Figma, A/B tested two against the live build.

Outcome. Median time-to-insight dropped from 8 min to under 2 min. Onboarding drop-offs fell 25%. Saved-view feature shipped to all tiers.

Kartly — E-commerce checkout

A 4-step checkout collapsed into 2, with address auto-detect, smarter defaults, and a payment step that actually fits on a phone. Killed the "guest vs. account" dead-end that was costing conversions.

Checkout conversion +18% E-commerce Mobile Conversion A/B

Senior UX · flows, prototypes, usability testing

Problem

Mobile cart abandonment was 71%. Heatmaps pointed at the address form and the account-gate. We needed a redesign that didn't scare the engineering team.

Process

A medication and appointment companion for chronic-care patients. Designed for older users on bad connections, with offline-first reminders and a one-tap "tell my doctor" flow that opens a call, not a form.
