Hi, I'm Himanshi.

UX Designer · Gurugram, India

"Good design shouldn't shout — it should whisper. The best interfaces are the ones you stop noticing because they get out of your way."

Who I am

I'm a UX designer with 3+ years of experience building scalable, data-driven products. I came into design through code — I spent five months as a backend developer before realising the part I loved was the seam between the system and the human using it. That dual lens shows up in my work: I think in components, but I design for the messy edges where real people live.

Today I lead end-to-end design at Dancing Numbers — research, IA, flows, hi-fi, design QA, and the design system that holds it all together.

What I do

I design for SaaS and data-heavy interfaces — the kind of products where users come to get a job done, not to be entertained. That means tight workflows, sensible defaults, and screens that respect your attention. I work closely with PMs and engineers; I prototype to make trade-offs visible early; I obsess about empty states.

Recent wins: reduced onboarding drop-offs 25% through flow optimisation, lifted daily active users 15% with a dashboard redesign, and cut design hand-off friction 20% by rebuilding the team's component library.

Off the clock

You'll find me hunting for coffee shops that get the foam right, re-organising my Figma libraries for the third time this week, or on a slow train somewhere in the hills. I keep a running list of tiny UX details I love — receipts, transit cards, museum signage — and study them like other people study films.

Agile Ventures — Website Redesign

Redesigned the corporate website for Agile Ventures Pvt. Ltd. — a Gurugram-based diversified business group. The goal was to modernise their digital presence, establish credibility across their multiple verticals, and make it easy for partners and clients to understand who they are and what they do.

Cleaner IA · Modern visual identity Web Redesign Corporate UI/UX Branding

Role

End-to-end UX/UI — research, IA, wireframes, visual design, prototype

Problem

The existing site didn't reflect the scale or credibility of the organisation. Navigation was unclear, the visual language was outdated, and the multiple business verticals had no coherent story tying them together.

Process

Audited the existing site and 5 competitor corporate sites. Ran stakeholder interviews to map business goals per vertical. Restructured the IA to lead with clarity — one clear homepage story, then individual vertical pages. Designed a clean, professional visual system in Figma and built an interactive prototype for sign-off.

Outcome. A modern, cohesive website that communicates Agile Ventures' breadth and professionalism at a glance. Simplified navigation reduced the steps to any key page from 4+ clicks to 2. The refreshed visual identity gave the brand a consistent, trustworthy feel across all verticals.

projects › dancing-numbers

Dancing Numbers — SaaS Website Redesign

Redesigned the website for Dancing Numbers — a QuickBooks automation SaaS trusted by 50,000+ accountants and bookkeepers across the USA. The challenge was translating a feature-heavy product into a website that communicates value instantly and drives free trial sign-ups without overwhelming first-time visitors.

Clearer conversion flow · Simplified IA SaaS Web Redesign Conversion UX B2B

Role

UI/UX Designer — working in-house as part of the Dancing Numbers product team. Responsible for end-to-end design: audit, IA, wireframes, visual UI, and developer handoff.

Problem

The existing site had deep, overlapping navigation across Services, Products, and Resources — making it hard for a first-time visitor to understand what Dancing Numbers actually does and why they should try it. The free trial CTA was buried, and the page structure didn't prioritise the core product value proposition.

Process

Audited the existing site across all key user journeys (accountant, small business owner, e-commerce). Ran a heuristic evaluation and mapped drop-off points. Rebuilt the IA around three audience segments with a clear homepage story: what it does → who it's for → why it works → start free trial. Designed new page layouts in Figma with a stronger visual hierarchy, cleaner typography, and prominent CTAs.

Outcome. A cleaner, faster-to-scan website that communicates the core value — bulk import, export, and delete for QuickBooks — within the first screen. Navigation simplified from 5 deeply nested menus to 3 clear sections. Free trial and demo CTAs elevated throughout, reducing friction for the primary conversion goal. The redesign supports a product trusted by 50,000+ users and over 10 million transactions processed.

projects › sleepsia

Sleepsia — D2C E-commerce Redesign

Redesigned the Shopify storefront for Sleepsia India — a Gurgaon-based D2C brand selling memory foam pillows, bedsheets, and sleep accessories. The brand sells across Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra, Blinkit, and Nykaa. The challenge was bringing the same trust and clarity to the owned website that the marketplace listings enjoy, and converting first-time visitors into buyers.

Cleaner PDP · Stronger homepage conversion E-commerce D2C Shopify Mobile-first

Role

UI/UX Designer — end-to-end: audit, user flows, wireframes, high-fidelity UI, mobile-first design, and Shopify theme handoff.

Problem

The existing site had a cluttered homepage with competing banners, a navigation with 7+ top-level categories and deeply nested sub-menus, and product pages that buried key trust signals (reviews, certifications, pain-relief benefits) below the fold. On mobile — where most Indian D2C traffic lands — the experience broke down significantly.

Process

Audited the existing site across homepage, category, and product pages. Mapped the customer's mental model: they arrive with a specific pain point (neck pain, back pain, pregnancy) not a product name. Rebuilt navigation around health concerns rather than product types. Redesigned the homepage to lead with the core promise and social proof. Rebuilt product pages to surface reviews, certifications, and the right-pillow-for-you content above the fold. Designed with a mobile-first approach throughout — Sleepsia's audience primarily shops on phones.

Outcome. A cleaner, faster-to-scan storefront built around the buyer's need — not the product catalogue. Navigation reduced from 7 top-level items to 3 clear entry points (Shop by Category, Shop by Concern, Deals). Product pages now surface trust signals — reviews, doctor recommendations, materials — within the first screen on mobile. The redesign supports a brand available on 14+ platforms and trusted by thousands of sleepers across India.

Lifestyle-first UX · Mobile-first Shopify Fashion D2C Shopify Women's Mobile-first

UI/UX Designer — brand direction, visual design, UX flows, Shopify theme design, mobile-first layout, and content strategy.

Sleepsia Lounge was a new sub-brand launching under the Sleepsia umbrella. Designed a homepage built around lifestyle imagery, the brand story ("More Than Nightwear — It's a Mood"), and a clear shop-by-style flow. Product pages led with fabric feel and styling context, with size and material details surfaced clearly. Cart and checkout optimised for one-thumb mobile use.

Outcome. A fully launched Shopify storefront with a distinct visual identity separate from the Sleepsia parent brand. The site communicates the brand's personality — "Breathe. Lounge. Designed the UI for GOPT — a healthcare web app that lets patients identify pain areas on an interactive 3D anatomy model, select symptom severity, and submit enquiries directly to doctors. Doctors then review, prescribe, and share reference videos via email — a seamless async consultation flow.

Intuitive 3D pain mapping · Clear consultation flow HealthcareWeb AppUX DesignFigma

Role

UI/UX Designer — user flows, interaction design, hi-fi UI, Figma prototype.

Problem

Describing pain to a doctor is inherently imprecise with text or dropdowns. The app needed an interface that allowed patients of varying digital literacy to pinpoint exactly where it hurts, how badly, and submit that clearly — without any clinical jargon.

Outcome. A clean, empathetic pain-reporting experience that removes the friction of describing symptoms in words — making healthcare more accessible and giving doctors richer, more precise patient data to work with.
